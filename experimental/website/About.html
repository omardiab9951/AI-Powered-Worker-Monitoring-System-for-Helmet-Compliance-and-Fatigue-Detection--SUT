About the Project & El Sewedy University

El Sewedy University (SUT)

A technology-focused university dedicated to applied engineering, hands-on research, and industry collaboration. SUT emphasizes practical projects that prepare students for real-world technical challenges.

This website and the AI systems showcased here are the result of a student-led capstone project within the Computer & Engineering departments at El Sewedy University. The teams focused on two safety-critical problems: helmet detection for personal protective equipment (PPE) compliance and fatigue detection for worker/driver safety.

Project Story & Inspiration

Our journey started with a simple observation: manual monitoring of safety compliance is error-prone and expensive. We wanted to build an accessible, low-cost solution that can help campus security and industrial partners improve safety without intrusive hardware changes.

The inspiration came from multiple sources — campus field visits, safety reports, and academic papers on vision-based PPE and drowsiness detection. We combined these insights with student research to curate the SUT El Sewedy University DataSet which captures realistic conditions such as occlusions, varying lighting, and different helmet styles.

What we built

  • Helmet detection pipeline — object detection model with bounding box output and confidence scores.
  • Fatigue detection pipeline — eye/mouth landmark analysis and temporal aggregation for drowsiness scoring (PERCLOS / yawning detection).
  • Frontend demo website — this site, providing upload/camera UI, demo flows, and a dashboard prototype for clients.

Related work & previous reports

During the research phase, we created and referenced several support sites and reports to document design decisions and experiments. Notable references used in the prior report include:

  1. University microsite redesign — UX/UI report
  2. Object detection experiments and model selection notes
  3. Edge deployment tests — latency & resource benchmarks

These reports guided our dataset collection, annotation standards, and the choices made for model architectures and preprocessing techniques.

Students & Supervisors

This project was implemented by students at El Sewedy University under academic supervision. The team carried out data collection on campus, annotation, model training and evaluation, and frontend/backend integration.

For inquiries or to request a pilot, please use: projectHelmet@sut.edu.eg

Privacy & Ethics

We take privacy seriously. This prototype is intended for pilot evaluation with informed consent. For any live deployment, we recommend a privacy impact assessment, clear signage, and on-device processing where possible to avoid sending raw video to the cloud.
